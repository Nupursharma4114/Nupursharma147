Paragraphs & Text Formatting

This is a normal paragraph describing the module. Use paragraphs to break up content into readable chunks.

Use bold to emphasise important concepts, italic for emphasis or titles, and underline for links or special emphasis when appropriate.

Combined formatting: Bold + Italic and inline code like <section> can appear inside paragraphs.

Special characters examples: ampersand (&amp;), less-than (&lt;), greater-than (&gt;), non-breaking space (&nbsp;), copyright (&copy;), trademark (&trade;), euro (&euro;).

Nested Lists

Nested lists are useful to show sub-items within a step or category.

  1. Module 1: Foundations
    • Topic A
    • Topic B
      1. Subtopic 1
      2. Subtopic 2
  2. Module 2: Applications

Mixed Lists

Mixing ordered and unordered lists gives structured flexibility:

  • Week 1
    1. Lecture: Introduction
    2. Lab: Setup environment
  • Week 2
    1. Lecture: Core concepts
    2. Assignment: Problem set 1
